Profile

A simulation-driven engineer who likes problems where the physics, the numerics, and the cost all have to be balanced at once.

I recently completed my Master’s in Aeronautical Engineering at Politecnico di Milano. My work spans the full simulation pipeline. I build the geometry and mesh, choosing the right turbulence and physics models, automating the solver runs, and then interrogating the results until they actually mean something.

Most of my projects live in external aerodynamics and turbulence (zonal RANS–DES wake studies, truck drag reduction, eVTOL rotor aeroacoustics), but I’m just as comfortable in controls and estimation, with robust H∞ synthesis, subspace system identification, and Monte Carlo validation. I also work in operations research and optimization, formulating LP/ILP/NLP, network, and metaheuristic models in AMPL for aerospace logistics and planning, and in hands-on design and manufacturing, from topology-optimized 3D-printed parts to sheet-metal mechanisms.

What ties it together is a habit of treating accuracy and computational cost as a single trade-off to be engineered, not assumed.

Zonal Methods for Near-Wake Prediction of the Ahmed Body

Master’s Thesis · Advisor Prof. Paolo Schito · 2024–25
Thesis

A hybrid RANS–DES “half-domain” strategy that resolves the near wake of a 25° Ahmed body at the fidelity of full-domain DES while cutting cost. A converged RANS field is sampled at mid-body length and becomes the inlet of a smaller DES domain, with a Synthetic Turbulence Generator (STG) rebuilding realistic fluctuations at the interface.

  • RANS converged to Cd = 0.296, within ~2% of experiment
  • Zonal run with STG matched full DES across the near wake; without STG the wake recovered too slowly
  • Polyhex-core mesh with a four-level mesh-independence study (4.8M cells)

eVTOL Aeroelasticity & Aeroacoustics Toolchain

High-fidelity simulation framework · case study
Individual

A coupled, fully automated simulation architecture for a 4-bladed variable-pitch tilt-rotor, proposed before geometry freeze. It targets two critical phenomena: transient aeroelastic stability through the hover→cruise tilt, and hover-phase tonal/broadband noise for urban compliance, and argues why the two physics problems must be decoupled rather than solved in one prohibitive run.

  • 2-way FSI (STAR-CCM+ ↔ NASTRAN) with overset meshes for tilt / spin / pitch and morphing for blade bending
  • URANS k-ω SST for loads; DES + Ffowcs-Williams–Hawkings for noise
  • End-to-end automation: OpenVSP Python API → Java macros → cluster orchestration, with CLS/RBF load–displacement mapping
  • Diagnosed an FSI vs experiment damping mismatch and proposed a low-cost triage path

System Identification of a UAV Multirotor

Estimation & Learning in Aerospace · Prof. Marco Lovera · 2024–25
Individual

Identified multirotor pitch dynamics three different ways (grey-box, hand-coded subspace, and a hybrid), then compared their fidelity and robustness under Monte Carlo.

  • Grey-box reached 94.9% FRF fit, and 99.96% on an independent 3-2-1-1 maneuver
  • Wrote PBSID subspace ID from scratch: Markov params → Hankel → SVD → state-space (76.9% VAF, stable poles)
  • Structured grey-box seeded from PBSID; 200-sample Monte Carlo on parameters, poles/zeros and FRF spread
